7 Signs You Need a New HVAC System (Don't Ignore #4)

Your HVAC system will give you warning signs before it completely fails. The problem is most homeowners don't know what to look for — until they're sweating through a Texas summer or freezing through an Oklahoma winter with no heat.

Sign #1: Your System Is Over 15 Years Old

The average HVAC system lifespan is 15–20 years for a central AC unit and 20–25 years for a furnace. If your system is approaching or past these ages, replacement is more cost-effective than continuing to repair an aging unit.

Sign #2: Rising Energy Bills

If your energy bills have been creeping up year over year without a change in usage, your HVAC system is likely losing efficiency. Older systems can lose 20–40% of their efficiency over time.

Sign #3: Frequent Repairs

The "5,000 rule" is a good guide: if the repair cost times the system's age exceeds $5,000, replacement is typically smarter than repair. For example, a $300 repair on a 20-year-old system = $6,000 — replace it.

The 5,000 Rule: Repair cost × System age. If the result exceeds 5,000, it's generally more cost-effective to replace the system.

Sign #4: Uneven Heating or Cooling (The One Most People Miss)

If some rooms are much hotter or colder than others, this signals your system can no longer distribute air properly. This could indicate ductwork issues, an undersized system, or a failing compressor — all expensive to ignore.

Sign #5: Strange Noises

Banging, clanking, or grinding sounds indicate mechanical problems. Squealing suggests belt or bearing issues. A properly functioning HVAC system should run quietly.

Sign #6: Humidity Problems

Your AC does double duty: cooling air AND removing humidity. If your home feels sticky even with the AC running, the system is struggling to dehumidify — a sign of declining capacity.

Sign #7: Using R-22 Refrigerant

If your AC was manufactured before 2010, it likely uses R-22 refrigerant, which has been phased out and is now extremely expensive. If your system needs a refrigerant refill, replacement is almost always more economical.

Repair vs. Replace: A Quick Guide

  • Repair: System is under 10 years old, cost is under $500, single component failure
  • Replace: System is over 15 years old, repair exceeds 50% of new unit cost, recurring problems

How Much Does HVAC Replacement Cost?

A full central HVAC system replacement (AC + furnace) typically costs $6,000–$12,000 installed. A central AC-only replacement runs $4,000–$8,000. The cost depends on your home size, efficiency rating (SEER), and your city.
